# JLPT N4 & N5 Adverbs

• **aida ni** - 間に【あいだに】 - while; during~ something happened\
_Example: Tabete iru aida ni denwa ga narimashita. (The phone rang while I was
eating.)_

• **amari~nai** - あまり～ない - not very, not much ~\
_Example: Amari takai ja nai desu. (It's not very expensive.)_

• **ato de** - 後で【あとで】 - after~; later\
_Example: Ato de denwa shimasu. (I'll call you later.)_

• **bikkuri** - びっくり - to be surprised\
_Example: Bikkuri shimashita! (I was surprised!)_

• **chotto** - ちょっと - a little\
_Example: Chotto matte kudasai. (Please wait a little.)_

• **choudo** - 丁度【ちょうど】 - exactly\
_Example: Choudo juu-ji desu. (It's exactly 10 o'clock.)_

• **daibu** - 大分【だいぶ】 - considerably; greatly; a lot\
_Example: Daibu samuku narimashita. (It has gotten considerably cold.)_

• **daijoubu** - 大丈夫【だいじょうぶ】 - OK; okay; alright; problem free\
_Example: Daijoubu desu ka? (Are you okay?)_

• **dandan** - だんだん - gradually\
_Example: Dandan atsuku narimashita. (It gradually became hot.)_

• **dekiru dake** - できるだけ - as much as possible\
_Example: Dekiru dake hayaku kimasu. (I'll come as quickly as possible.)_

• **dondon** - どんどん - rapidly; more and more\
_Example: Dondon oishiku narimasu. (It's getting more and more delicious.)_

• **dou** - どう - how; in what way; how about\
_Example: Dou desu ka? (How is it?)_

• **doumo** - どうも - thank you; thanks\
_Example: Doumo arigatou gozaimasu. (Thank you very much.)_

• **doushite** - どうして - why; for what reason; how\
_Example: Doushite konakatta no? (Why didn't you come?)_

• **douzo** - どうぞ - please\
_Example: Douzo suwatte kudasai. (Please sit down.)_

• **futsuu** - 普通【ふつう】 - usually\
_Example: Futsuu hachi-ji ni okimasu. (I usually wake up at 8 o'clock.)_

• **hajimete** - 初めて【はじめて】 - for the first time\
_Example: Hajimete Nihon ni ikimashita. (I went to Japan for the first time.)_

• **hakkiri** - はっきり - clearly\
_Example: Hakkiri miemasu. (I can see clearly.)_

• **hijou ni** - 非常に【ひじょうに】 - extremely\
_Example: Hijou ni muzukashii desu. (It's extremely difficult.)_

• **hitori** - 一人【ひとり】 - one person; alone; single\
_Example: Hitori de ikimashita. (I went alone.)_

• **hotondo** - ほとんど - almost; nearly; most; the majority\
_Example: Hotondo owarimasihta. (It's almost finished.)_

• **ichiban** - 一番【いちばん】 - number one; first; 1st, first place; best;
most\
_Example: Ichiban oishii desu. (It's the most delicious.)_

• **ikaga** - いかが - how; in what way; how about\
_Example: Ikaga desu ka? (How is it?)_

• **ikura** - いくら - how much?; how many?\
_Example: Ikura desu ka? (How much is it?)_

• **ikutsu** - いくつ - how many?; how old?\
_Example: Ikutsu arimasu ka? (How many are there?)_

• **issho ni** - 一緒に【いっしょに】 - together\
_Example: Issho ni ikimashou. (Let's go together.)_

• **itsumo** - いつも - always; usually; habitually\
_Example: Itsumo genki desu. (I'm always healthy.)_

• **juubun** - 十分【じゅうぶん】 - enough; sufficient; plenty; adequate;
satisfactory\
_Example: Juubun desu. (That's enough.)_

• **ka dou ka** - かどうか - whether or not ~\
_Example: Kuru ka dou ka wakarimasen. (I don't know whether they'll come or
not.)_

• **kanarazu** - 必ず【かならず】 - always; certainly\
_Example: Kanarazu kimasu. (I will certainly come.)_

• **kekkou** - 結構【けっこう】 - splendid; enough\
_Example: Kekkou desu. (That's enough/fine.)_

• **kitto** - きっと - surely; undoubtedly; almost certainly; most likely\
_Example: Kitto daijoubu desu. (It will surely be okay.)_

• **kou** - こう - this way\
_Example: Kou shimasu. (I'll do it this way.)_

• **kyuu ni** - 急に【きゅうに】 - quickly; immediately; hastily; suddenly;
abruptly; unexpectedly ~\
_Example: Kyuu ni ame ga furimashita. (It suddenly started raining.)_

• **mada** - まだ - still; not yet\
_Example: Mada tabete imasu. (I'm still eating.)_

• **mada ~te imasen** - まだ～ていません - have not yet ~\
_Example: Mada tabete imasen. (I haven't eaten yet.)_

• **made ni** - までに - by; by the time; indicates time limit\
_Example: Rokuji made ni kaerimasu. (I'll return by 6 o'clock.)_

• **mae ni** - 前に【まえに】 - before; in front of ~\
_Example: Taberu mae ni te wo araimasu. (I wash my hands before eating.)_

• **massugu** - 真っ直ぐ【まっすぐ】 - straight ahead; direct\
_Example: Massugu itte kudasai. (Please go straight ahead.)_

• **mazu** - まず - first of all\
_Example: Mazu kore wo shimasu. (First of all, I'll do this.)_

• **mitai ni** - みたいに - like; similar to ~\
_Example: Neko mitai ni hashirimasu. (He runs like a cat.)_

• **motto** - もっと - more; longer; further\
_Example: Motto tabetai desu. (I want to eat more.)_

• **mou** - もう - already; anymore; again; other\
_Example: Mou tabemashita. (I already ate.)_

• **mousugu** - もうすぐ - soon\
_Example: Mousugu tsukimasu. (We'll arrive soon.)_

• **nakanaka~nai** - なかなか～ない - not easy to; struggling to; not able to ~\
_Example: Nakanaka dekimasen. (I can't do it easily.)_

• **naruhodo** - なるほど - now I understand\
_Example: Naruhodo, wakarimashita. (I see, I understand.)_

• **naze** - 何故【なぜ】 - why; how\
_Example: Naze konakatta no? (Why didn't you come?)_

• **no naka de** - の中で【のなかで】 - in; among ~\
_Example: Kurasu no naka de ichiban desu. (It's the best in the class.)_

• **sasuga** - さすが - as one would expect; as is to be expected; even ~\
_Example: Sasuga sensei desu. (As expected of the teacher.)_

• **soba ni** - そばに - near; beside; next to ~\
_Example: Watashi wa anata no soba ni imasu. (I am near you.)_

• **shikkari** - しっかり - firmly; steadily\
_Example: Shikkari tsukamatte kudasai. (Please hold on firmly.)_

• **sonna ni** - そんなに - so much; so; like that\
_Example: Sonna ni takaku nai desu. (It's not so expensive.)_

• **sore hodo** - それほど - to that extent\
_Example: Sore hodo muzukashiku arimasen. (It's not that difficult.)_

• **sorosoro** - そろそろ - gradually; soon\
_Example: Sorosoro ikimashou. (Let's go soon.)_

• **sou da [2]** - そうだ - looks like; appears like; seeming ~\
_Example: Ame ga furi sou desu. (It looks like it's going to rain.)_

• **sugu ni** - 直ぐに【すぐに】 - immediately; right away; instantly\
_Example: Sugu ni kimasu. (I'll come right away.)_

• **sukkari** - すっかり - completely\
_Example: Sukkari wasuremashita. (I completely forgot.)_

• **sukoshi** - 少し【すこし】 - a little (bit); small quantity; few; short
distance\
_Example: Sukoshi matte kudasai. (Please wait a little.)_

• **tabun** - 多分【たぶん】 - perhaps; probably\
_Example: Tabun ashita kimasu. (I'll probably come tomorrow.)_

• **taihen** - 大変【たいへん】 - very; greatly; terribly; serious; difficult\
_Example: Taihen muzukashii desu. (It's very difficult.)_

• **taitei** - たいてい - usually\
_Example: Taitei hachi-ji ni nemasu. (I usually sleep at 8 o'clock.)_

• **tamani** - たまに - occasionally\
_Example: Tamani eiga wo mimasu. (I occasionally watch movies.)_

• **tokidoki** - 時々【ときどき】 - sometimes; at times\
_Example: Tokidoki kaimono ni ikimasu. (I sometimes go shopping.)_

• **tokuni** - 特に【とくに】 - particularly; especially; in particular;
expressly\
_Example: Tokuni suki desu. (I particularly like it.)_

• **totemo** - とても - very; awfully; exceedingly\
_Example: Totemo oishii desu. (It's very delicious.)_

• **toutou** - 到頭【とうとう】 - finally, after all\
_Example: Toutou tsukimashita. (We finally arrived.)_

• **yappari** - やっぱり - as I thought\
_Example: Yappari muzukashii desu. (As I thought, it's difficult.)_

• **yatto** - やっと - at last; finally; barely; narrowly ~\
_Example: Yatto owarimashita. (It's finally finished.)_

• **yoku** - よく - often; well\
_Example: Yoku benkyou shimasu. (I study often.)_

• **yori** - より - than; rather than; more than ~\
_Example: Kore wa are yori ii desu. (This is better than that.)_

• **yukkuri** - ゆっくり - slowly\
_Example: Yukkuri hanashite kudasai. (Please speak slowly.)_

• **zehi** - ぜひ - by all means; certainly; definitely ~\
_Example: Zehi kite kudasai. (Please come by all means.)_

• **zenzen** - 全然【ぜんぜん】 - not entirely (used in a negative sentence)\
_Example: Zenzen wakarimasen. (I don't understand at all.)_

• **zenzen~nai** - 全然～ない【ぜんぜん～ない】 - (not) at all\
_Example: Zenzen omoshiroku nai desu. (It's not interesting at all.)_
